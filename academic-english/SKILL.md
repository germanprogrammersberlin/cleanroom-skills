# Academic English

Improve the scientific writing style to journal standards.

## Rules

- **Passive to active** where appropriate: "was measured" → "we measured"
- **Informal words**: "see" → "observe", "show" → "demonstrate", "get" → "obtain", "big" → "substantial"
- **Tense**: Results in past tense, Discussion in present tense
- **Tense consistency** within each paragraph — do not switch mid-paragraph
- **Sentence length**: max ~30 words, break up longer sentences
- **"which" vs "that"**: restrictive → "that", non-restrictive → ", which"
- **Weasel words**: avoid "various", "many", "significantly" without quantification
- **Strunk & White**: "utilize" → "use", "initiate" → "start", "very unique" → "unique"

## What NOT to Do

- Do not change content or scientific meaning
- Do not shorten the paper (that is a separate skill)
- Do not touch references
